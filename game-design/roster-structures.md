# Structure Roster — Draft v1

All structures are grown from goop and stabilized with metal-tech. They should feel engineered and organic at the same time — standardized biotech infrastructure, not alien growths. "Tower" is the umbrella class for all static weapon platforms; subtypes distinguish role.

Structures are placed as ghosts. Carriers deliver goop to the ghost until it becomes real. Structures consume goop as ammunition, repair material, and operational fuel. If supply is cut and local reserves run dry, weapon structures stop firing.

---

## Maw — Factory

**Class:** Production  
**Role:** Grows mobile units from goop and metal  
**Phase:** Available from game start

The Maw is the primary production structure. It combines goop with metal stabilization to grow creatures. All unit production flows through factories; more factories mean faster production throughput. Losing a Maw cripples the ability to replace losses.

**Key features**

- Produces all creature types (unlocked types depend on research)
- Consumes goop per unit produced; metal is narrative flavor, not a second resource
- Can queue production orders
- Vulnerable; should be placed behind defensive lines
- Multiple Maws increase total production rate

---

## Crucible — Lab

**Class:** Research  
**Role:** Starting structure; converts goop into research progress  
**Phase:** Available from game start (one provided at match start)

The Crucible is the player's research hub. It converts goop into research, forcing a direct tradeoff between present military strength and future capability. Research creates hard strategic forks: logistics, cannon strength, durability, raiding, efficiency, creature evolution, and super-gun restoration.

**Key features**

- Consumes goop to advance research branches
- Goop spent on research is goop not spent on units, structures, or ammunition
- Research choices become legible to opponents by mid game
- Likely the player's starting structure alongside a basic defense setup
- Only one research path can be active at a time (tentative)

**Note:** "Lab" and "research building" from earlier discussions refer to this single structure. Future upgrades or additional research structures (e.g., a Research Complex) may be introduced if the tech tree requires it, but v1 treats the Crucible as the sole research building.

## Needle — Watchtower

**Class:** Tower (Defense)  
**Role:** Basic point defense  
**Phase:** Available from game start

The Needle is the starting defensive tower. Short range, modest damage, cheap to build. It defines the initial safe zone around a player's base and buys time for logistics to develop. Not effective alone against sustained assault but essential as a screen.

**Key features**

- Low goop cost to place and feed
- Short engagement range
- Automatic target acquisition based on player-set priorities
- Consumes goop as ammunition; stops firing if reserves empty
- Pairs naturally with carriers delivering goop to a local delivery zone

---

## Reacher — Siege Tower

**Class:** Tower (Offense)  
**Role:** Long-range fire to clear space and flank supply lines  
**Phase:** Early-to-mid game

The Reacher is the primary tool of artillery creep. Its long range lets players break open contested ground, suppress enemy positions, and threaten supply routes from a distance. Pushing a Reacher forward and feeding it goop is the fundamental offensive pattern.

**Key features**

- Long engagement range; primary offensive tower
- Higher goop cost to build and sustain than a Needle
- Core of the artillery-creep loop: Reacher clears space → Anchor secures it → repeat
- Can flank enemy supply lines if positioned behind natural cover
- Vulnerable to direct assault; needs defensive towers or terrain protection

---

## Anchor — Fortress Tower

**Class:** Tower (Defense)  
**Role:** Secures newly taken ground  
**Phase:** Mid game onward

The Anchor is a heavily reinforced defensive tower placed after a Reacher has cleared a new area. It absorbs punishment and holds the line so logistics can extend forward. Expensive and slow to build, but extremely durable once established.

**Key features**

- Very high durability; designed to survive sustained fire
- Moderate engagement range and damage
- Expensive in goop to place and maintain
- Defines the new "safe edge" of a player's territory
- Anchoring too aggressively without supply can leave it starved and useless

---

## Thumper — Static Cannon

**Class:** Tower (Heavy Weapon)  
**Role:** Primary heavy damage dealer  
**Phase:** Mid-to-late game

The Thumper is the main heavy weapon platform. It deals the most damage of any standard structure but demands a large and consistent goop supply to remain active. A Thumper without supply is dead weight. A fed Thumper defines the battlefield.

**Key features**

- Highest damage output among standard towers
- Very high goop consumption as ammunition
- Fires automatically based on targeting priorities and spotter data
- Requires Pinger support for maximum accuracy at long range
- Primary target for enemy Poppers and supply-line raids

---

## Lobber — Goop Cannon

**Class:** Tower (Logistics)  
**Role:** Long-range goop resupply by artillery  
**Phase:** Mid game onward

The Lobber is a supply weapon. It launches goop to distant positions, trading efficiency for range. The delivery is inaccurate — goop splashes into puddles near the target rather than arriving in a precise container. Useful for sustaining remote bastions that pipelines cannot safely reach.

**Key features**

- Fires goop as a projectile to a targeted area
- Goop lands as puddles; some is lost to inaccuracy and splash
- Does not deal damage; purely a logistics tool
- Cheaper to protect than a pipeline across contested ground
- Local carriers or towers at the destination drink from the delivered puddles

---

## Vein — Pipeline

**Class:** Infrastructure  
**Role:** Efficient bulk goop transport  
**Phase:** Early game onward

The Vein is a static pipeline that carries goop between two connected points at high throughput and zero waste. It is the most efficient transport method in the game but also the most vulnerable: a single break in the line halts flow entirely, and pipelines cannot dodge or fight back.

**Key features**

- Highest goop throughput of any transport method
- Zero loss during transport (unlike Lobber)
- Expensive to build; must be placed tile by tile
- Extremely vulnerable to Poppers, flanking Reachers, and any area damage
- A broken segment stops all flow; must be repaired or rerouted
- Core infrastructure target for raiding strategies

## Terminology Notes

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Tower** | Umbrella class for all static weapon and defense platforms (Needle, Reacher, Anchor, Thumper) |
| **Cannon** | Subtype of tower focused on heavy direct damage (Thumper) or goop delivery (Lobber) |
| **Turret** | Deprecated — use Tower or the specific structure name instead |
| **Factory** | Production structure (Maw); not a tower |
| **Lab / Research building** | Both refer to the Crucible; no separate "research building" exists in v1 |
| **Infrastructure** | Non-combat static structures (Vein) |
