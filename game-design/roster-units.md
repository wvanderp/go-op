# Unit Roster — Draft v1

All mobile units are domesticated goop-creatures stabilized by metal-tech. They are bred utility animals first and military assets second. Silhouettes should be rounded, readable, and somewhat appealing.

"Carrier" is a creature class, not a single unit. Collecting and delivering are command-roles assigned through zones and priorities, not separate unit types.

---

## Gobbet — Carrier (Light)

**Class:** Carrier  
**Role:** Basic goop transport  
**Phase:** Available from game start

The Gobbet is the backbone of every logistics network. A small, rounded creature bred for steady walking and reliable delivery. It picks up goop at collect zones and deposits it at delivery zones according to player-set priority. Cheap to produce and expendable, but individually slow and low-capacity.

**Key features**

- Carries a small amount of goop per trip
- Automatically routes between collect and delivery zones
- Responds to priority and flow-map guidance
- Can drink from nearby goop puddles when idle
- Dies easily to any combat threat

---

## Trundle — Carrier (Heavy)

**Class:** Carrier  
**Role:** High-volume goop transport  
**Phase:** Early-to-mid game production

The Trundle is a larger transport creature that hauls significantly more goop per trip than a Gobbet. Slower, more expensive, and a bigger target, but essential once logistics distances grow and throughput matters more than unit count.

**Key features**

- Carries substantially more goop than a Gobbet
- Slower movement speed, larger silhouette
- Higher production cost in goop
- Same zone-based collect/deliver behavior as all carriers
- Worth protecting; losing a loaded Trundle spills a visible puddle

---

## Peeper — Scout

**Class:** Recon  
**Role:** Fog-of-war removal  
**Phase:** Available from game start

The Peeper is a fast, fragile creature with eyes adapted for long-range terrain scanning. It lifts fog of war in a radius around itself, feeding vision data into the shared targeting network. Cheap and disposable, but critical for enabling weapon systems to acquire targets.

**Key features**

- Fast movement speed
- Wide vision radius relative to its cost
- Very fragile; dies to almost any threat
- Targets it reveals become available to the entire weapon network
- Height advantage extends its sight range

---

## Pinger — Spotter

**Class:** Recon  
**Role:** Persistent detection and targeting  
**Phase:** Early-to-mid game

The Pinger provides sustained detection and targeting data for the weapon network. Unlike the Peeper, which moves fast and sees broadly, the Pinger deploys in place and provides higher-fidelity target information that improves weapon accuracy or enables specific targeting modes.

**Key features**

- Deploys stationary to provide a detection field
- Feeds targeting data to cannons and towers within network range
- More durable than a Peeper but immobile when deployed
- Can be redeployed by picking up and moving
- Essential for long-range cannon accuracy against specific targets

---

## Popper — Sapper

**Class:** Raider  
**Role:** Logistics disruption and infrastructure destruction  
**Phase:** Mid game onward

The Popper is a fully controllable explosive creature. Its fuse ignites the moment it is produced, giving the player direct control over a ticking weapon. Poppers are used to cut supply lines, destroy pipelines, and break through defended positions. They do not return; their role is sacrifice.

**Key features**

- Fuse starts on spawn; limited lifespan
- Fully player-controlled movement (no automation)
- Explodes on contact with target or when fuse expires
- High damage in a small area; effective against pipelines and carriers
- Cannot be queued or automated; demands active micro

---

## Bristle — Carrier (Armed)

**Class:** Carrier  
**Role:** Self-defense transport (later evolution)  
**Phase:** Late game; requires research

The Bristle is an evolved carrier variant bred with basic deterrent capability. It can fend off light harassment while hauling goop, reducing the need for dedicated escorts on dangerous routes. Not a combat unit; its armament is defensive and weak compared to static weapons.

**Key features**

- Carries goop like a standard carrier
- Has a short-range defensive attack against nearby threats
- More expensive than a Gobbet, less capacity than a Trundle
- Requires a specific research branch to unlock
- Fills the gap between undefended carriers and full military escorts

---

## Terminology Notes

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Carrier** | Creature class encompassing all goop-transport units (Gobbet, Trundle, Bristle) |
| **Collector** | Not a unit type. Collecting is a behavior assigned when a carrier operates in a collect zone |
| **Recon** | Creature class for vision and detection units (Peeper, Pinger) |
| **Raider** | Creature class for offensive disposable units (Popper) |
