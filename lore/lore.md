# Lore Foundation

## Setting

The battlefield should feel like ordinary Earth: fields, forests, roads, and intact natural terrain. The war machine is disturbing because it intrudes on a recognizable landscape rather than because the world is already corrupted. The countryside stays readable because local ecosystems recover faster than the war scars them, even when the same regions are contested repeatedly.

## Conflict

The conflict is a corporate concession war. Multiple licensed operators hold overlapping extraction rights over the same agricultural region, its buried goop reserves, and the dormant infrastructure beneath it. On paper the dispute is about contracts, transport corridors, and utility control. In practice it is a private war fought with domesticated biotech.

## The Nature of Goop

Goop is a thick, glossy, semi-transparent fluid with a dark oily red color. It reads as both biological and manufactured because it is not purely an invention: it is a native planetary organism or secretion that humanity learned to cultivate, refine, and direct. In fiction, goop is not just fuel. It is the universal substrate from which power, ammunition, repairs, structures, and living units all emerge.

## Living Industry

Industry turned goop into a controllable labor ecosystem. Factories combine goop with metal stabilization and grow obedient utility animals that can self-route, self-balance, and survive rough terrain better than conventional machinery. The result is a bio-industrial supply chain that feels halfway between herd management and automated war production.

## Creature Identity

The creatures should be more animal-like than human. They are engineered and useful, but not grotesque. Their shapes should be rounded, readable, and somewhat cute because they were bred for calm behavior and operational legibility, not intimidation. Even specialized military variants should still read as militarized utility life rather than monsters.

## Power Goop

Power goop is geologically matured goop. It forms where ordinary goop has spent decades under heat, pressure, and mineral contact, which makes it denser, more energetic, and less stable than the common form. That is why it appears in contested central ground and why advanced systems, restored relics, and endgame technology depend on it.

## Battlefield Relics

The dormant super-guns are repurposed terraforming hardware. They were originally built to reshape weather, irrigation, and soil chemistry at enormous scale, then converted into artillery once civil infrastructure and military escalation became inseparable. Reactivating them means feeding old world-shaping machinery back into the war economy.

## The Player

The player is the distributed intelligence that emerges once enough goop reserves, sensors, towers, and living units are linked together. The clinical interface is not just a stylistic layer. It is how the network perceives the battlefield and exerts control over it.

## Command Fantasy

The player experience is clinical and deliberate. Even though the battlefield is wet, organic, and industrial, the interface presents it like a cold control problem. That contrast between visceral war material and sterile command language is a major part of the game's identity.
